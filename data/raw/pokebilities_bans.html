What is Pokebilities AAA?​
Pokebilities AAA, often shortened to PokeAAA, is the mashup of Pokebilities and Almost Any Ability. In this format, all of a Pokemon's obtainable base abilities are active simultaneously, plus one additional chosen ability, in the same vein as AAA.

Ruleset and Banlist:
Species Clause: A player cannot have two Pokemon with the same National Pokédex number on a team.
OHKO Clause: A Pokemon may not have the moves Fissure, Guillotine, Horn Drill, or Sheer Cold in its moveset.
Evasion Items Clause: A Pokemon may not hold Bright Powder.
Evasion Moves Clause: A Pokemon may not have either Double Team or Minimize in its moveset.
Sleep Moves Clause: Bans all moves that induce sleep.
Endless Battle Clause: Players cannot intentionally prevent an opponent from being able to end the game without forfeiting.
Single Ability Clause: Two Pokemon on the same team may not share an ability. This does not apply to base abilities granted by Pokebilities.
Regenerator Clause: No more than two Pokemon per team may have the ability Regenerator.

Frequently Asked Questions
Gameplay:
Q. How do the abilities get added to the Pokemon?
A. In the teambuilder, select your "chosen ability" in the ability slot. Your Pokemon will have its regular "base" abilities added in battle.

Q. Where can I play PokeAAA?
A. A ladder is available on Pokemon Showdown! under the "Other Metagames" category. Room tournaments and discussions are held in the OM Mashups subroom as well.

Tiering:
Q. Why are there so many complex bans?
A. Due to the nature of the format and all other formats where a Pokemon can have multiple active abilities, it is healthier to restrict broken combinations of abilities (like Primordial Sea and Swift Swim, or Hadron Engine and Quark Drive) instead of banning the Pokemon or the individual abilities, which are not broken by themselves.

Q. Why are Pokemon with banned abilities (Magnezone) or banned ability combinations (Ninetales-Alola) hard banned?
A. Per Ivy's post in the Pokebilities thread, modifying the mechanics of an Other Metagame to provide leeway for otherwise broken Pokemon goes against the spirit of Other Metagames. Other Metagame Mashups do not mechanically deviate from their Other Metagame counterparts except for occasions when mechanics must be modified to be functional. HiZo has outlined the arguments here.

Q. Why is Regenerator Clause in effect?
A. Without it, Regenerator cores consisting of multiple base users and an AAA user dominate the metagame and are able to (effectively) infinitely stall other teams that cannot break through their cores.

Mechanics:
Pokemon Formes:
Q. How does Ditto work?
A. Imposter will copy all of the opponent's abilities. Ditto's chosen ability does work, but is overwritten by Imposter upon activation. The only relevant ability is Magic Guard, although Intimidate and Weather-setting abilities do activate and might have some niche usage. Additionally - if the copied Pokemon has an ability that announces itself (Pressure, Sword of Ruin, etc.), Ditto will not be able to scout that Pokemon's chosen ability.

Q. How does Ash-Greninja work?
A. Ash-Greninja is a separate Pokemon from regular Greninja. Greninja cannot use Protean and Battle Bond simultaneously, it must pick between having Protean and Torrent as base abilities, or Battle Bond as a base ability. Battle Bond works as normal and Ash-Greninja can use a chosen ability as well.

Q. How does Morpeko work?
A. Morpeko's Hunger Switch is not affected by the mechanics of PokeAAA and will behave as normal.

Q. How does Palafin work?
A. Base form Palafin will have its chosen ability active. If Palafin is holding an Ability Shield or has Zero-To-Hero in the ability slot, Zero-to-Hero will activate upon the second switch-in as normal, but it will not have its chosen ability active. Without Ability Shield, Zero-to-Hero will not activate. It is reccomended to use Zero-To-Hero as the chosen ability, as Palafin gains little from having its base form use an extra ability.

Q. How does Terapagos work?
A. Terapagos's chosen ability will be active on switch-in (similar to Ditto). Terapagos will immediately transform into Terapagos-Terastal, which will only have Tera Shell active.

Q. How does Zamazenta-Crowned work?
A. Zamazenta-Crowned's chosen abilities will not activate.
Ability Interactions:
Q. How does Trace work?
A. Trace copies a random ability.
Q. How do Lingering Aroma, Mummy, and Wandering Spirit Work?
A. These abilities only affect the opponent's chosen ability. If a Pokemon with these abilities makes contact with an opposing user Lingering Aroma/Mummy/Wandering Spirit user, it will also have its chosen ability replaced with Lingering Aroma/Mummy/Wandering Spirit.

The following interactions are what happens when a Pokemon uses these abilities on the same set:
Protosynthesis and Quark Drive do not stack, and the affected stat will only be boosted by 30%
Mummy and Wandering Spirit will only affect the chosen ability
Poison Touch, Stench, and Toxic Chain do not interact with Sheer Force
Corrosion allows Poison Point, Poison Touch, and Toxic Chain to poison Steel and Poison types. Toxic Spikes are unaffected.
Plus and Minus do not interact with each other
Inner Focus stops Intimidate's attack drop, and Defiant will not activate
Inner Focus prevents Flinching, and Steadfast will not activate unless the opponent has Mold Breaker.
Magic Guard does not prevent the damage caused by Clangorous Soul or breaking a Disguise
Gale Wings does not boost the priority of Aerilate-boosted Normal moves
If a Libero/Protean Pokemon uses an -ate ability, clicking an -ate boosted Normal move will change its type to Normal

Certain moves interact with abilities unconventionally:
Gastro Acid and Worry Seed will only affect the chosen ability
Steely Spirit boosts the power of Doom Desire, as it is technically an ally's Steel-type move
Skill Swap will only swap the chosen abilities. It will still fail if a Pokemon has Protosynthesis, Quark Drive, or other untransferable/unsupressable abilities as either base or chosen abilities.
Please reach out if you have any more questions or clarifications!

Hello again, it's time for a PokeAAA update! The tier has seen a noticeable increase in play and we've been able to figure some things out about balance and tiering! First things first:

Cloyster has been banned!
Cloyster's base ability of Skill Link forms a devastating combo with Technician, granting it a whopping 187.5 base power Icicle Spear and Rock Blast. With this gigantic power boost, Cloyster could even run a significant investment into its special attack to target the few physical walls able to stand up to it after a shell smash.

Several ability combinations are being restricted:
Sand Stream and Sand Rush
Snow Warning and Slush Rush
As one of the only formats where Pokemon can have more than one active ability, PokeAAA has always needed to make some harsh restrictions to remain balanced. A notable historical example was the combination of a weather setting ability and a weather-dependent speed boosting ability, which immediately doubled the user's speed and often provided a power boost to their STAB or coverage moves. This combination is broken on a single Pokemon, but has counterplay and significant opportunity cost while restricted, as it forces teams to give up multiple slots for a combination easily disrupted by primal weather. Sand Stream + Sand Rush and Snow Warning + Slush Rush initially slipped under our radar, as they don't provide a power boost and are limited to a smaller pool of Pokemon, but have proven that they are much like their sun and rain counterparts. Simply put, the mechanic of doubling a Pokemon's speed without setup or additional team support is broken.
Sand Stream and Sand Veil
Snow Warning and Snow Cloak
On the other hand, we all know Evasion boosting is fundamentally broken. In most other formats, running a team with a Snow Cloak or Sand Veil user is suboptimal and almost always not worth the opportunity cost. In PokeAAA, a Pokemon is able to set the condition and abuse the evasion boost without a teammate setting weather or being forced to manually set weather. Allowing these Pokemon to have an evasion chance significantly higher than brightpowder is unfair and uncompetitive, and as a result will be restricted from the tier. Unfortunately, Vulpix-Alola and Ninetales-Alola have the combination of Snow Warning and Snow Cloak as base abilities, and are not useable in the tier like last generation.

The Weather Rocks are all unbanned!
Now that the most problematic aspects of weather have all been dealt with, we are testing the presence of the weather extension stones in the tier. Weather is not in a great spot, and teams relying on weather are easily and harshly disrupted by common users of Desolate Land and Primordial Sea. Weather rocks were banned in Pokebilities, where primordial weather isn't available, and are not broken in this format.

Thanks for playing, and I hope to play against you in the PS! room sometime!

Thank you to everyone who participated in the PokeAAA Kickoff Tour! This helped develop the metagame and it seems like we're in a healthy place, with one exception:

Stored Power is now banned from PokeAAA!

We noticed one thing during the tournament: the best win conditions in the tier are the Stored Power sets on Hatterene and Reuniclus. Their base abilities get around the critical weaknesses of Stored Power sweepers (passivity and vulnerability to status), and with the addition of Stamina, these Pokemon can come in on a weak hit, get a defense boost, and proceed to set up and win the game. There's another unfair win condition that abuses Stored Power as well: Polteageist's normal Queenly Majesty set is notably buffed by the addition of Weak Armor as a base ability, which is able to add 40bp to Stored Power and enables +4 Polteageist to outspeed most of the scarfers in the tier.

Hatterene, Polteageist, Reuniclus, and Stamina are not broken on their own. They have several other sets that can be used effectively and their presence is overall healthy for the tier. Stored Power is what pushes these Pokemon over the edge, and the council has decided to take action on this move to preserve other portions of the metagame.

Other than that, we're glad to have seen so much play over the past few weeks. We'd love it if you sent in some teams to be added to our samples list. Thanks for playing!
